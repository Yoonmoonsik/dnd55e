## 📜 Class Features

### Level 1 — The Foundation

> *The first bullet you fire will tell enemies everything they need to know. Make it count.*

**🎯 Firearm Expert**
You've spent your life with a gun in your hand. You ignore the Loading property of firearms, you don't have disadvantage on attack rolls while within 5 feet of a hostile creature, and being prone doesn't impose disadvantage on your ranged attacks.

**⚡ Quick Draw**
When combat begins, you're already ready. You can draw or stow a firearm as a free object interaction, and add your proficiency bonus to Initiative rolls.

**🥊 Fighting Style**
Choose one fighting style to define your approach to combat. Your style reflects the gunfighter you are — not just how you shoot, but how you *think*.

**⚔ Weapon Mastery**
Your training with firearms goes beyond mechanics — it's instinct. Choose two firearms whose Mastery properties you can apply when you attack with them. You can change your Mastery choices on a Long Rest.

---

### Level 2 — The Edge

> *Knowing when to pull the trigger is half the battle. Knowing when not to is the other half.*

**💀 Critical Shot**
When you score a Critical Hit with a firearm, the target must make a Constitution saving throw or suffer an additional effect — a Gut Wound, a Shattered Limb, or worse. The battlefield is yours to control.

**🎲 Risk**
Once per turn, before you make an attack roll, you can push your luck. Roll a d6 — on a 4 or higher, you add the result to your attack roll and damage. On a 1-3, you subtract it. Fortune favors the bold... sometimes.

---

### Level 4 — Ability Score Improvement

Increase one Ability Score by 2, or two Ability Scores by 1 each. Alternatively, take a Feat.

---

### Level 5 — The Sharpshooter

> *Two shots. Two dead. One problem solved.*

**🔫 Extra Attack**
You can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

**🩸 Gut Shot**
When you hit a creature with a firearm attack, you can choose to forgo some of your damage to Gut Shot the target — reducing their speed and potentially causing them to retch, losing their next action.

---

### Level 7 — The Ghost

**💨 Evasion**
When you are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, you instead take no damage on a success and only half damage on a failure. You can't use this feature while Incapacitated.

---

### Level 11 — The Legend

**☠ Overkill**
When you reduce a creature to 0 hit points with a firearm attack, the excess damage carries over to another creature of your choice within range. The battlefield has no safe corners when you're shooting.

## 🎲 High Roller
*"The house always wins — because I'm the house."*

A gambler, a risk-taker, a calculated chaos agent. The High Roller doesn't just fight — they play the odds, manipulate fortune, and walk away richer from every encounter. Whether at the card table or the battlefield, they always have one more trick up their sleeve.

| Level | Feature |
|-------|---------|
| 3 | **Poker Face** — You've learned to read people the way others read books. Gain proficiency in Insight and Deception. When you make a Charisma (Deception) check, add your Wisdom modifier as a bonus. |
| 3 | **Liar's Dice** — Once per Short Rest, you can force a creature that can see you to make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, they are Charmed by you until the end of your next turn. |
| 6 | **Risky Business** — Your Risk feature now adds a d8 instead of a d6, and on a result of 1, you no longer subtract — you simply add nothing. The house rules are yours now. |
| 10 | **Risk Taker** — When you use Risk and roll a 4 or higher, you can make one additional firearm attack as a Bonus Action. You live for the moment the odds flip in your favor. |

## ✨ Spellslinger
*"Why choose between magic and bullets when you can have both?"*

Not all Gunslingers walk the mundane path. The Spellslinger has unlocked the secret that no wizard dares admit: arcane energy and gunpowder are the same force, just channeled differently. They weave spells into every shot, turning firearms into focuses and bullets into spell delivery systems.

| Level | Feature |
|-------|---------|
| 3 | **Spellcasting** — You gain the ability to cast spells, using Intelligence as your spellcasting ability. Your firearm is your spellcasting focus. |
| 3 | **Bang, You're Dead!** — When you cast a spell that requires a spell attack roll, you can make it through your firearm instead. On a hit, the spell deals its damage *and* your firearm's damage. |
| 6 | **Spellshot** — Once per turn when you hit a creature with a firearm attack, you can expend a spell slot to deal extra force damage equal to 1d8 per spell slot level. The magic and the metal are one. |
| 10 | **Counter-Mage** — When a creature you can see casts a spell, you can use your Reaction to make a firearm attack against them. On a hit, they must make a Constitution saving throw or their spell fails and the spell slot is wasted. |

## ⚖ White Hat
*"There are rules. You broke them. I'm here to collect."*

The White Hat is the law incarnate — a relentless, principled Gunslinger who has taken it upon themselves to enforce order in a chaotic world. Where others see a battlefield, they see a crime scene. Where others see enemies, they see defendants. Justice isn't just their motivation — it's their weapon.

| Level | Feature |
|-------|---------|
| 3 | **Lay Down the Law** — You have advantage on Charisma (Intimidation) checks against creatures who have attacked you or your allies in the last minute. Your reputation precedes you. |
| 3 | **Steely-Eyed** — You cannot be Frightened. Additionally, when a creature attempts to Charm you, you can make a Wisdom saving throw to resist the effect even if it would normally not allow one. |
| 6 | **Reach for the Skies** — As a Bonus Action, you can issue a lawful warning to a creature within 30 feet. They must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or spend their next turn with their hands raised, unable to take actions or reactions. |
| 10 | **Long Arm of the Law** — Your firearm attacks ignore Half Cover and Three-Quarters Cover. Additionally, creatures you've marked as targets cannot benefit from the Help action from other creatures. There is no hiding from justice. |
